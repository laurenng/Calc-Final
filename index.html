Food + Math = :)

For my second project, I wanted to do something artsy. However, I can't draw or do anything artsy. But I can cook, to some extent... So I decided to make myself lunch and attempt do math.

Ingredients

I was feeling really hungry that day, so I decided to raid my fridge and pantry. These are some of the items I found. Actually, it was more like 10% of the total items I used. (Hopefully my mom doesn't realize that there's a lot of food missing)

Preparation

Preparation was tedious. I spent about 3 thinking about what I wanted to make. I wanted dessert, a cheese platter, pasta, ice cream... Couldn't pick 1 thing. So I it all

Actually making it look nice

Cooking the food only took about an hour. But making it look nice and somewhat apetizing was a struggle I didn't know if I wanted to make it simplistic or rustic or just pure randomness. So the presentation took about 2 hours.

logistic cheese platter

To start our meal, I present to you my award winning cheese platter with pepperjack cheese, mozzarella, red walnuts, seasame crackers, mickey shaped goldfish, chocolate, and bell peppers.

Slope Field Pasta Salad

For our main course, I present to you a pasta salad. This is a balsamic glazed pasta salad with carmelized onions, mozzarella, hand-picked basil and locally sourced bell peppers. It is representing a slope field of y=x

DESSERTTT!!!!

This is probably my favorite course, because there's sprinkles. It is a buiscuit with marscapone cream cheese frosting, a mixed berry compote, and a fruit salad. The expodential curve is made up of the buscuit and marshmallows. To show it goes on to infinity, I arranged the fruit to look like it blew up.
